1. CORE PROBLEM

What specific problem are you solving?
• Be specific and measurable
• Focus on one core problem
• Describe the current state vs desired state

2. TARGET CUSTOMER

Who exactly faces this problem?
• Demographics: Age, income, location
• Behavior: How they currently solve it
• Psychographics: Values, motivations

3. PROPOSED SOLUTION

How does your product solve the problem?
• Core features (max 3)
• How it works (simple steps)
• What makes it different

4. PAIN INTENSITY

Rate the pain level (1-10):
□ Frequency: How often? ___/10
□ Urgency: How urgent? ___/10
□ Cost: Current cost? ₹_____
□ Emotion: How frustrating? ___/10

5. CURRENT ALTERNATIVES

How do people solve this today?
• Direct competitors
• Indirect competitors
• Manual processes
• No solution (they suffer)

6. UNIQUE VALUE PROPOSITION

Why you vs alternatives?
• 10x better? 10x cheaper? 10x easier?
• What can't competitors copy?
• Your unfair advantage

Problem-Solution Fit Scorecard

RED FLAGS (Stop & Pivot):

  • □ Pain score below 7/10
  • □ Less than 70% confirm problem exists
  • □ Can't find 100 potential customers
  • □ Existing solutions are "good enough"
  • □ No one willing to pay

GREEN LIGHTS (Move Forward):

  • □ Pain score 8+/10
  • □ 80%+ confirm problem urgency
  • □ Clear target customer segment
  • □ 60%+ would pay for solution
  • □ You can build it in 3 months

Next Steps (Day 2-3 Actions):

□ Interview 20 target customers - Use customer interview script
□ Create landing page - Test demand with email signups
□ Build simple prototype - Paper, slides, or mockup
□ Research competition - Complete competitor analysis
□ Calculate market size - TAM/SAM/SOM analysis
