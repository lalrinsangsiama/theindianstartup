Term Sheet Negotiation Overview

🎯 What is a Term Sheet?
A term sheet is a non-binding document that outlines the basic terms and conditions of an investment. It serves as a foundation for the legal investment documents and covers key aspects like valuation, equity structure, governance, and investor rights.

🏆 Key Success Principles

  • Preparation is Everything: Understand every term before negotiations begin
  • Focus on Value: Negotiate terms that maximize long-term value, not just valuation
  • Win-Win Mindset: Build relationships, not just close deals
  • Know Your BATNA: Always have alternatives and walk-away scenarios

⏱️ Negotiation Timeline
Phase 1: Initial Term Sheet (1-2 weeks)
Negotiate core economic terms - valuation, investment amount, equity percentage Phase 2: Detailed Terms (1-2 weeks)
Work through governance, protection, and control provisions Phase 3: Final Terms (3-5 days)
Finalize remaining items and prepare for legal documentation

🚨 Red Flags to Watch

  • Participating Liquidation: Investors get money back PLUS pro-rata share
  • Full Ratchet Anti-dilution: 100% protection for investors in down rounds
  • Excessive Board Control: Investors controlling all major decisions
  • Broad Reserved Matters: Too many decisions requiring investor approval
  • Founder Vesting Acceleration: No acceleration on termination/acquisition

💡 Valuation Negotiation Tips

  • Benchmark Against Comparables: Research similar companies' valuations at similar stages
  • Use Multiple Metrics: Revenue multiples, user metrics, growth rates
  • Highlight Growth Potential: Focus on future opportunity, not just current metrics
  • Create Competition: Multiple interested investors increase leverage
  • Be Realistic: Overvaluation can hurt future rounds
🎯 Option Pool Impact Important
The option pool is typically carved out of the pre-money valuation, meaning founders bear the full dilution cost. This "option pool shuffle" can significantly impact founder ownership.
Scenario Pre-money Option Pool Investment Founder %
No Option Pool ₹40 Cr 0% ₹10 Cr 80%
15% Option Pool (Pre-money) ₹40 Cr 15% ₹10 Cr 68%
15% Option Pool (Post-money) ₹47 Cr 15% ₹10 Cr 72%

🏆 Option Pool Best Practices

  • Negotiate Pool Size: Start with smaller pool (10-12%), expand as needed
  • Carve from Post-money: Try to get option pool from post-money valuation
  • Staged Creation: Create pool in tranches as you hire
  • Use Existing Pool: If you have unused options, count them toward new pool

Equity Structure & Rights

💧 Liquidation Preferences Critical
Liquidation preferences determine the order and amount investors receive in an exit event (sale, liquidation, or IPO).
Preference Type How it Works Founder Impact Recommendation
1x Non-participating Investor gets 1x investment OR pro-rata share (whichever higher) Fair for founders ✅ Accept
1x Participating Investor gets 1x investment AND pro-rata share Double-dipping by investor ❌ Avoid
2x+ Multiple Investor gets 2x+ investment before others Severely limits founder returns ❌ Avoid

🔒 Anti-dilution Provisions Important
Anti-dilution provisions protect investors from dilution in down rounds by adjusting their conversion price.
Protection Type How it Works Founder Impact Acceptability
Broad-based Weighted Average Includes all securities in calculation Lower founder dilution ✅ Acceptable
Narrow-based Weighted Average Only includes common/preferred stock Moderate founder dilution ⚠️ Negotiate
Full Ratchet Conversion price drops to new round price Massive founder dilution ❌ Avoid

🚨 Anti-dilution Red Flags

  • Full Ratchet Provisions: Can destroy founder ownership in down rounds
  • No Carve-outs: Protection should exclude option grants and other normal issuances
  • Long Duration: Protection should sunset after 2-3 rounds

Board Control & Governance

🎯 Board Composition Critical
Board composition determines who controls key company decisions. It's often more important than ownership percentage.
Board Structure Composition Control Recommended Stage
Founder Controlled 2 Founders, 1 Investor, 1 Independent Founder majority Seed to Series A
Balanced Board 2 Founders, 2 Investors, 1 Independent Independent decides Series A to B
Investor Controlled 1 Founder, 2+ Investors, 1+ Independent Investor majority Avoid if possible

🏆 Board Control Best Practices

  • Maintain Founder Control: As long as possible, especially early stages
  • Choose Independents Carefully: They often become tie-breakers
  • CEO Board Seat: Ensure CEO always has a board seat
  • Board Observer Rights: Allow other investors as observers
📋 Reserved Matters (Protective Provisions) Important
Reserved matters are decisions that require investor approval, regardless of board or shareholder control.

Standard Reserved Matters

💡 Negotiating Reserved Matters

  • Keep List Short: Limit to truly important decisions
  • Set High Thresholds: Only major transactions need approval
  • Include Time Limits: Auto-approval if investors don't respond
  • Ordinary Course Exception: Normal business operations shouldn't need approval

Investor Protection Rights

👥 Drag-along & Tag-along Rights Moderate
These provisions govern how shares can be sold and ensure liquidity for all shareholders.
Right Type Purpose How it Works Impact on Founders
Drag-along Force minority to sell Majority can force all shareholders to sell Can be forced to sell
Tag-along Protect minority rights Right to join any sale by majority Provides liquidity protection

🏆 Negotiation Points

  • Drag-along Threshold: Require supermajority (75%+) to trigger
  • Fair Price Protection: Sale price must meet minimum thresholds
  • Tag-along Coverage: Should include all founder shares
  • Notice Periods: Adequate time to consider offers
🔄 Right of First Refusal (ROFR) & Co-sale Moderate
ROFR gives the company and investors the right to purchase shares before they're sold to third parties.

💡 ROFR Negotiation Tips

  • Carve-out Family Transfers: Exempt transfers to family members
  • Reasonable Exercise Period: 30 days maximum to exercise rights
  • Co-sale Rights: If company waives ROFR, investors can co-sell
  • Sunset Provisions: ROFR should end after IPO or certain time
📊 Information Rights Moderate
Information rights ensure investors receive regular updates on company performance and operations.

Standard Information Rights

💡 Managing Information Rights

  • Standardize Reporting: Create templates to reduce workload
  • Reasonable Frequency: Monthly financials, quarterly deep dives
  • Confidentiality: Ensure investors maintain confidentiality
  • Sunset for Small Investors: Rights should end if ownership drops below threshold

Situation: Investor wants 1.5x participating liquidation preference. This means they get 1.5x their money back AND pro-rata share in any exit.

Your Response:

Accept to close the deal quickly
Counter with 1x non-participating
Offer 1x participating with cap at 3x return
Explain why this hurts founder incentives
Scenario 3: Board Control Discussion

Situation: You currently have 3-person board (2 founders, 1 investor). New investor wants 2 investor seats, making it 2 founders, 2 investors, 1 independent.

Your Response:

Accept the balanced board
Propose 2 founders, 1 existing investor, 1 new investor
Offer board observer seat instead
Increase to 7 people: 3 founders, 2 investors, 2 independents

🚨 Red Flags Checklist

Never Accept These Terms

  • ❌ Participating Liquidation Preference: Double-dipping by investors
  • ❌ Multiple Liquidation Preference (2x+): Severely limits founder returns
  • ❌ Full Ratchet Anti-dilution: Transfers all down-round risk to founders
  • ❌ No Founder Vesting Acceleration: Founders lose equity on termination
  • ❌ Investor-Controlled Board: Founders lose control of their company
  • ❌ Broad Reserved Matters: Every decision needs investor approval
  • ❌ Personal Guarantees: Founders personally liable for company obligations
  • ❌ Right to Replace CEO: Investors can remove founder CEO without cause

Always Negotiate For

  • ✅ 1x Non-participating Liquidation: Fair to all parties
  • ✅ Weighted Average Anti-dilution: Reasonable protection for investors
  • ✅ Founder-Friendly Board: Maintain control as long as possible
  • ✅ Acceleration on Termination: 12 months acceleration if fired without cause
  • ✅ Acceleration on Sale: Full acceleration if company is sold
  • ✅ Reasonable Reserved Matters: Only truly important decisions
  • ✅ Pro-rata Rights: Right to maintain ownership in future rounds
  • ✅ Information Rights: Regular reporting to keep investors informed
